## Batch Filming Workflow

### Film Day (2x per week recommended)

**Monday: Film Tuesday-Thursday content**
**Thursday: Film Friday-Sunday + Monday content**

Each filming session (~90 minutes):
1. Set up phone on tripod with ring light (10 min)
2. Review scripts for the batch (10 min)
3. Film all talking-head segments back-to-back (20 min)
4. Film all screen recordings back-to-back (20 min)
5. Quick rough edits — cuts only, add text overlays (30 min)

### Screen Recording Setup
- iPhone: Settings → Control Center → add Screen Recording
- Hide notifications: Settings → Focus → Do Not Disturb (during recording)
- Full charge or plugged in (screen recording drains battery)
- Close all apps except CreatorAI
- Pre-load prompts you'll use in the demo

### Filming Tips
- Natural lighting > ring light > overhead light
- Hold phone at eye level (tripod or stack of books)
- Film in 9:16 vertical (TikTok native)
- Record 3 takes of each hook — pick the most natural one
- Don't script word-for-word — memorize key points, ad-lib the rest

## Scheduling Tools

| Tool | Free Tier | Best For |
|------|-----------|---------|
| TikTok Native Scheduler | Yes (built-in) | Simple scheduling, no extra tools |
| Later | Yes (limited) | Multi-platform scheduling |
| Buffer | Yes (limited) | Analytics + scheduling |
| Hootsuite | No | Enterprise, team workflows |

**Recommendation:** Start with TikTok's native scheduler (free, reliable), upgrade to Later or Buffer when posting 2+ platforms.
